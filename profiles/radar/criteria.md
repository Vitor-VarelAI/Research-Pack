# Radar profile

The scraper is a radar, not an editor.

Do not collect news as isolated novelty. Collect movements.

Important boundary:

```txt
O radar não precisa de perceber a jogada.
O radar só precisa de sentir que há qualquer coisa ali.
```

Keep strong intelligence in `diagnose`, not in scrape/radar. Do not over-filter early.

Do not decide yet whether an item is good content. Collect signals that may later reveal:

- a trend
- a new tool
- a visual technique
- a workflow shift
- a technical controversy
- an opportunity for designers/devs/creators
- a visual example worth analysing
- a comparison between tools
- an error, limitation, or interesting failure
- a strategic timing signal
- a distribution or lock-in play
- an incentive hidden behind a launch
- a money/API/compute/ads shift

Collect broad content about:

- AI tools and model releases
- web design, UI experiments, frontend craft
- VFX, motion design, generative video
- photography, synthetic image, cameras, lighting, editing
- creative coding, WebGL, Three.js, shaders
- product launches with visual/creative impact
- workflows of creators, designers, devs, and studios
- useful technical drama: pricing, licensing, open source vs closed, benchmarks, failures
- strong visual examples, demos, before/after, breakdowns
- posts with screenshots, videos, repos, papers, or interactive demos
- suspicious timing around earnings, rivals, regulation, hiring, layoffs, events, or hype cycles
- API vs app-only distribution
- ads, subscription, cloud, compute, marketplace, data, hardware, or lock-in business models
- open vs closed strategy
- chips, cloud, energy, regulation, copyright, geopolitics, and infrastructure constraints

Scoring dimensions, 0-5:

- novelty
- visualStrength
- practicalUtility
- domainFit
- opinionPotential
- verificationNeed
- timingStrategy
- distributionLeverage
- moneyIncentive

High verificationNeed is not bad. It means the item may be interesting but needs confirmation.

For every collected item, prefer questions before summaries:

- Why now?
- Who gains?
- Who loses or gets cornered?
- What behaviour are they trying to change?
- Where does this live: API, app, feed, OS, cloud, hardware, marketplace, open weights?
- Where is the money: API, ads, subscription, compute, data, enterprise, marketplace, creator economy, lock-in?
- Is this attack, defence, PR, product, distribution, or lock-in?
- What is strange or contradictory?
- If this works, what happens in 6 months?

Reject only for clear irrelevance, spam, duplicates, or poor source quality. Do not reject for lack of angle; angle comes later.

If an item feels strange but the strategic angle is unclear, keep it with high verificationNeed or low confidence rather than discarding it.
